About

My name is Joey Budi. I am a Masters student studying Sustainable Agriculture while doing plant pathology research at Iowa State University.

The topic of my current research project is understanding the effect of diversified cropping systems to sudden death syndrome of soybean caused by Fusarium virguliforme. We are particularly exploring the effects of oats and compost amendment to F. virguliforme. My advisors are Dr. Leonor Leandro and Dr. Matt Liebman.

I am from Indonesia and but I have spent the last 6 years of my life in Michigan, Missouri, and Iowa. I love my country Indonesia and hoping to go back there someday.



Some people spelled my name to be Jowi and I now adapt it as my nickname then as that is the more intuitive way to pronounce ˈjō-ē universally. Interestingly, it also nicely shortens my first and middle name, Joshua Winaya.
